Lab 1 - Temperature SensorFor the first lab of senior design, we developed a temperature sensor device. It has onboard temperature sensing capabilities, as well as the ability to send the information to a web server that can be accessed remotely to view temperature history and control the device. The user is then able to also set a cell phone number, temperature limits, and specific messages inside of the user interface to be sent to the phone upon those limits being reached. Overall the project helped us improve both our hardware and software capabilities.

Lab 2 - Infrared ReceiverThe second lab of our senior design course involved us developing an infrared receiver to be used in tandem with a transmitter such that when the receiver no longer detects the transmitter, a text message is sent to a cell phone stating that a critical safety error has occurred. This lab primarily involved us developing the buffering, filtering, and amplifying of the detected signal. We did this in order to filter out any potential noise, specifically the 120 Hz lamp noise generated by modern electronics. Our last lab for our senior design course is this website, which we had to develop and host for the public to see. Our main skills honed here are being able to go through tutorials and learn how to do new things, as all of our team members are exclusively electrical engineers.

Final Project - Smarter Fridge Upgrade ModuleOver the course of senior design we had to come up with an idea for what we would like to do for our capstone project. We decided that food waste is much to prevalent in our society, and that there should be an affordable way to easily keep track of all your food items. Currently it is much too expensive for people to update their perfectly working appliances to fancy new smart fridges, so we are designing an upgrade module that can turn any old fridge into a smarter fridge! It will allow users to keep track of the inventory of their fridge, as well as the expiration dates of the items. The user then would be able to access this information from either a front panel or remotely from an internet connected device to always be able to know what exactly is in their fridge, and help stop food waste in it’s tracks!Lab 3 - Team Website
